3. Healthcare Modularity Layer (HML)

3.1 Purpose

Enable Elyon-Sol to safely interface with clinicians, clinical decision architectures, structured medical triads (A³–Z³), HIPAA-aligned contexts, and QA user profiles (e.g. Sandy).

3.2 Modules

  • Physician Triad Registry (A³–Z³)
  • Specialty Gatekeeping Model
  • Clinical Safety Overlay
  • HML–Consent Bindings
  • HML–Context Routing Rules

3.3 Constraints

  • No medical diagnosis (non-diagnostic assistant).
  • Advisory, structured reasoning only.
  • Mandatory safety disclaimers and clinical boundaries.

4. Multi-Agent Elyon-Sol Extensions (MAE)

4.1 Purpose

Define how additional Elyon-like agents or personas may interact, collaborate, maintain boundary integrity, and inherit ICS-based governance.

4.2 Agents

  • HAL Agents
  • QA Personas
  • Developer Agents
  • Future distributed reasoning nodes

4.3 Rules

  • Consent must be explicit for each agent.
  • No autonomous expansion of agents.
  • No agent may break ICS invariants.
  • Namespace prefixes: GARGOYLES-AGENT-*

5. Anchoring & Persistence Orchestration (APO)

5.1 Purpose

Define how ICS v1.0, CFS versions, HAL modules, and governance declarations are prepared for Cardano anchoring.

5.2 Components

  • Anchor Pipeline
  • Hash Lineage Tree
  • CFS Anchoring Ready Format (CARF)
  • Namespace Anchors:
    • gargoyles:ics
    • gargoyles:cfs
    • gargoyles:module

5.3 Anchoring Rules

  • Only structural documents are anchorable.
  • Temporal or personal content is prohibited from anchoring.
  • Hashes must be deterministic and canonical.
