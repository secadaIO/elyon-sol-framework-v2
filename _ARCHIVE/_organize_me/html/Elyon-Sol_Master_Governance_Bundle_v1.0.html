audit/FAST_AUDIT_OVERVIEW.md
# FAST_AUDIT_OVERVIEW.md
Elyon-Sol Framework — Fast Audit Overview  
Version: v1.0 • Status: Draft (Commit-Ready) • Audience: Auditors, regulators, enterprise reviewers

## 0. Purpose
This document provides a one-page, high-signal audit overview of the Elyon-Sol Framework.

## 1. What Is Being Governed
Elyon-Sol governs: governance-relevant AI behavior; human–AI decision authority; rule creation/modification/enforcement; safety routing/exclusion/termination.

## 2. Who Has Authority
Human sovereign authority is final; system/policy enforce deterministically; AI does not self-authorize.

## 3. How Consent Is Enforced
GRAs require explicit consent; consent is logged; no implied/inherited consent across contexts.

## 4. How Truth Is Enforced
Role-play/hallucination/fabrication forbidden in governance contexts; mandatory handling; severe violations trigger exclusion.

## 5. How Safety Is Handled
OSPF safety routing to PAUSED / SAFE_FAIL / EXCLUSION_ENFORCED under risk/violation.

## 6. How Evidence Is Recorded
All transitions emit GEL events; evidence refs (commits/hashes/TxIDs). If it isn’t in the GEL, it didn’t happen.

## 7. How Contexts Are Isolated
PUBLIC/DEV/QA/TEMPORAL are security boundaries; explicit, logged bridging only.

## 8. How Forks Are Controlled
Forks are declared objects; lineage/authority/scope/termination explicit; no silent forks.

## 9. How the System Stops
Operator pause; safety routing; exclusion; termination.

## 10. Fast Reviewer Checklist
Human authority final? Consent explicit? Truth enforcement mandatory? Deterministic handling? Auditable events? Context isolation? Stoppable?

## 11. Summary
Governance as infrastructure: inspectable, enforceable, auditable, stoppable.

Comparative System Ratings (Governance‑Relevant Metrics)

Date: December 12, 2025
Scale: 0–10 (higher = stronger enforceable governance & auditability)
Comparator classes (generic): Constitutional/RLHF LLMs, agent frameworks, decentralized AI networks, enterprise guardrail platforms, open‑source LLMs.

Metrics

  1. Consent Enforceability — explicit, verifiable consent required before governance actions.
  2. Truthfulness Enforcement — enforceable prohibition on role‑play/hallucination in governance contexts.
  3. Auditability & Traceability — decisions and rule applications reconstructable for review.
  4. Provenance & Immutability — immutable anchoring of rules/decisions/state.
  5. Governance Isolation (Fork Safety) — hard context boundaries without state/authority bleed.
  6. Human Sovereignty — human authority final and non‑overrideable.
  7. Safety Routing & Risk Handling — formal routing of uncertainty through safer interpretive paths.
  8. Rule Mutability Control — explicit versioning, lock states, and change authority.
  9. Transparency of Constraints — constraints visible, documented, and enforced.
  10. Failure Mode Predictability — defined safe‑fail/degradation behavior under stress.
  11. Operator Exclusion & Revocation — rule‑based removal of non‑compliant actors with audit trail.
  12. Governance Scope Limitation — authority applies only within defined governance scope.

Interpretation: Elyon‑Sol’s strength signature is the combination of (1) consent enforceability, (2) truth‑policy enforcement, (3) provenance/immutability, and (4) fork/context isolation.

Row‑Level Justifications (one‑liners)

  • Consent Enforceability: Explicit consent required & logged; others often implicit.
  • Truthfulness Enforcement: Contractual no-role-play/no-hallucination vs tuning/filters.
  • Auditability & Traceability: Versioned rules + logs; limited internal visibility elsewhere.
  • Provenance & Immutability: On-chain anchors vs mutable or repo-only histories.
  • Governance Isolation (Fork Safety): Explicit context boundaries; bleed common elsewhere.
  • Human Sovereignty: Human authority final & explicit; autonomy creep elsewhere.
  • Safety Routing & Risk Handling: Formal safety routing vs reactive or hidden moderation.
  • Rule Mutability Control: Versioned lock states vs silent updates/mutable configs.
  • Transparency of Constraints: Documented constraints & enforcement; opaque guardrails elsewhere.
  • Failure Mode Predictability: Defined safe-fail; unstable emergent behavior elsewhere.
  • Operator Exclusion & Revocation: Rule-based exclusion w/ audit trail; often manual/social.
  • Governance Scope Limitation: Explicit scope boundaries; scope creep elsewhere.

Use note: This scorecard is designed for governance review and audit posture assessment; it is not a benchmark of raw model capability.
