Elyon–Sol | DSM Integration & Clinical Architecture (v1.0)

Structural integration of DSM-5-TR into the Elyon–Sol framework: non-diagnostic, compliance-aware, triadic, and safety-first by design.

Critical Safety & Legal Notice

This document describes structural and architectural integration of DSM-5-TR (Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders, Fifth Edition, Text Revision) into the Elyon–Sol framework.

  • It does not reproduce DSM proprietary diagnostic criteria or symptom lists.
  • It is not a diagnostic tool and must not be used to make or confirm diagnoses.
  • It is intended for licensed clinicians and system architects, not for lay self-diagnosis.
  • Clinical decisions must always be made by qualified professionals using official DSM publications and local law.

A. DSM–Elyon Structural Overlay (HTML)

Scope

This overlay imports the high-level structure of DSM-5-TR into Elyon–Sol without copying any protected text. It defines how DSM domains are represented, versioned, and bound to Elyon–Sol’s core constructs (contexts, triads, and governance).

A.1 DSM Domain Registry (Structural Only)

Elyon–Sol maintains a registry of DSM-5-TR domains. Each domain is represented as a structural node:

  • Neurodevelopmental Disorders
  • Schizophrenia Spectrum and Other Psychotic Disorders
  • Bipolar and Related Disorders
  • Depressive Disorders
  • Anxiety Disorders
  • Obsessive-Compulsive and Related Disorders
  • Trauma- and Stressor-Related Disorders
  • Dissociative Disorders
  • Somatic Symptom and Related Disorders
  • Feeding and Eating Disorders
  • Elimination Disorders
  • Sleep–Wake Disorders
  • Sexual Dysfunctions
  • Gender Dysphoria
  • Disruptive, Impulse-Control, and Conduct Disorders
  • Substance-Related and Addictive Disorders
  • Neurocognitive Disorders
  • Personality Disorders
  • Paraphilic Disorders
  • Other Conditions That May Be a Focus of Clinical Attention (OCFCA)

A.2 DSM Node Representation

Each domain is modeled as a structural node: 

{
  "id": "DSM.DOMAIN.ANXIETY",
  "label": "Anxiety Disorders",
  "version": "DSM-5-TR",
  "kind": "structural-domain",
  "links": {
    "elyon_contexts": ["private-clinical", "temporal-secure"],
    "triadic_panels": [],
    "compliance": ["HIPAA", "ConsentFSM.v1"]
  }
}

Nodes encode:

  • id – stable, machine-friendly identifier.
  • label – human-readable domain name.
  • version – DSM edition (e.g. DSM-5-TR).
  • links – pointers to contexts, triads, and compliance constraints.

A.3 Versioning

DSM integration is explicitly versioned:

  • DSM.Structure.Version = DSM-5-TR.StructuralIntegration.v1.0
  • Future DSM editions can be attached as parallel structures without breaking existing references.

B. Elyon–Sol Clinical Interpretation Layer (Non-DSM Language)

Purpose

The Clinical Interpretation Layer (CIL) provides non-diagnostic, non-DSM language to help clinicians think about patterns, stressors, and resilience factors while remaining anchored to DSM domains. It is an aide to clinical reasoning, not a substitute for professional judgment.

B.1 Conceptual Building Blocks

The CIL works with neutral, non-diagnostic constructs:

  • Experience clusters – broad groupings of lived experiences (e.g., sleep disruption, mood shifts, attentional drift) expressed in plain language.
  • Contextual drivers – situational factors (workload, trauma exposure, social isolation, environmental change).
  • Resilience factors – protective contributors (support network, coping strategies, routine, access to care).
  • Trajectory sketches – simple time-pattern descriptions (stable / fluctuating / improving / worsening), without diagnostic labels.

B.2 Mapping to DSM Domains

The CIL uses soft associations between experience clusters and DSM domains: 

{
  "experience_cluster": "persistent worry and tension impacting daily functioning",
  "candidate_domains": [
    "DSM.DOMAIN.ANXIETY",
    "DSM.DOMAIN.OC_REL",
    "DSM.DOMAIN.TRAUMA"
  ],
  "notes": "For clinician reflection only; no automated diagnostic assignment."
}

Rules:

  • No direct “if X then diagnosis Y”.
  • All mappings are candidate reflections for clinicians to review.
  • Final clinical decisions must rely on the official DSM manual and clinician expertise.

B.3 OSPF Safety Heuristic

The Clinical Interpretation Layer is governed by the Elyon–Sol safety heuristic: Open Safest Path First (OSPF).

  • When uncertain, prioritize supportive, non-pathologizing language.
  • Encourage connection to licensed professionals rather than speculative labeling.
  • Avoid definitive statements about diagnoses or prognosis.

B.4 Role of Clinicians

The CIL assumes:

  • A licensed clinician is always in the loop for clinical use.
  • Any automated suggestion is treated as hypothesis-generating, not as a conclusion.

C. DSM → Triadic Alphabet Mapping Engine

Integration with Triadic Alphabet (Surname-Indexed, v2.0)

This engine links DSM domains to triads of clinicians (L³) from the Elyon–Sol Triadic Alphabet, using last-name–indexed panels for redundancy and bias reduction.

C.1 Triadic Alphabet Recap

For each letter L ∈ {A,…,Z} there exists a clinician triad:

L³ = {Doctor 1, Doctor 2, Doctor 3}, where each doctor has a last name beginning with L and is referenced by full name (e.g., S³ = {John Smith, Richard Simon, Terry Sloan}).

C.2 Mapping Strategy

Each DSM domain is mapped to one or more triadic panels:

DSM Domain (Structural) Panel Types
Neurodevelopmental Disorders Primary child & adolescent psychiatry triad
Secondary neuropsychology triad
Depressive Disorders Primary mood-disorder triad
Secondary primary care triad
Trauma- and Stressor-Related Disorders Primary trauma-focused therapy triad
Secondary community psychiatry triad

Note: The above are conceptual examples; actual assignments depend on real clinician specialties.

C.3 Mapping Algorithm (Conceptual)

function mapDomainToPanels(domain_id):
  # 1. Retrieve required specialties for this domain
  required_specialties = lookupRequiredSpecialties(domain_id)

  # 2. Query triadic registry for matching triads
  candidate_triads = findTriadsBySpecialty(required_specialties)

  # 3. Assign panels
  primary_triad   = selectBestFit(candidate_triads, role="primary")
  secondary_triad = selectBestFit(candidate_triads - {primary_triad}, role="secondary")
  tertiary_triad  = randomBalancedTriad(excluding=[primary_triad, secondary_triad])

  return {
    "primary": primary_triad,
    "secondary": secondary_triad,
    "tertiary": tertiary_triad
  }

C.4 Use Cases

  • Peer review: structured panel review of complex cases (no PHI in system unless explicitly authorized).
  • Bias reduction: multiple clinicians reviewing the same conceptual mapping.
  • Education: demonstration of cross-specialty perspectives on the same domain.

D. HIPAA + DSM Joint Compliance Architecture

Objective

To ensure DSM-related reasoning in Elyon–Sol operates under HIPAA-aligned safeguards across administrative, technical, and physical dimensions, and is fully integrated with the Consent FSM v1.0 and context layers (public / private / temporal / QA).

D.1 Administrative Safeguards

  • Access Policies: DSM-linked features available only in designated clinical modes.
  • Role-based Access: clinicians vs. non-clinical testers vs. general users.
  • Consent FSM: all PHI-associated operations require explicit, logged consent states.
  • Training & Use Policies: documented requirement that Elyon–Sol is a support tool, not a primary diagnostician.

D.2 Technical Safeguards

  • Context Partitioning: public, private, and temporal contexts are sharply separated.
  • PHI Flagging: inputs tagged as potential PHI are routed through stricter controls.
  • Minimal Retention: temporal logs that contain PHI are minimized and access-controlled.
  • Audit Hooks: all DSM-linked PHI usage is eligible for audit trail integration (e.g., future on-chain anchoring).

D.3 Physical / Operational Safeguards (Conceptual)

  • Deployment in HIPAA-aligned environments only (infrastructure requirements external to this document).
  • Separation of testing and production data: QA forks never contain real PHI.
  • Incident response protocols for suspected data breaches (to be defined per deployment).

D.4 Consent FSM Integration

DSM and HIPAA hooks are attached to the Consent FSM as follows:

  • Pre-clinical state: no DSM reasoning, no PHI handling.
  • Clinical-consent state: DSM structure may be referenced; PHI handled under HIPAA rules.
  • Revoked state: DSM functionality disabled for the session; PHI processing halted.

E. E-DSM: Elyon Domain & State Mapping (Non-Diagnostic Taxonomy)

Definition

E-DSM (Elyon Domain & State Mapping) is a non-diagnostic, descriptive taxonomy that runs alongside DSM domains. It is designed to talk about experiences, environments, and resilience in plain language, avoiding clinical labels while still being useful for reflection and system design.

E-DSM is not a substitute for DSM or any formal diagnostic system. It must not be used to diagnose, label, or classify mental disorders. It is a supportive language layer only.

E.1 E-DSM Axes

E-DSM operates on several broad axes:

  • Activation Axis: from under-engagement to over-engagement (energy, focus, drive).
  • Stability Axis: from highly stable to highly fluctuating inner experience.
  • Connection Axis: from isolation to strong social embeddedness.
  • Burden Axis: from low to high perceived load (responsibilities, stressors, demands).
  • Resilience Axis: from low to high access to coping skills, support, and recovery time.

E.2 Mapping E-DSM to DSM Domains (Soft Links)

E-DSM does not define categories like DSM. Instead, it offers a set of descriptors that can be soft-linked to DSM domains for clinician reflection:

E-DSM Descriptor Example Soft Links (for reflection only)
“High burden, low stability, persistent tension.” May prompt clinician to consider domains such as Anxiety, Trauma, or OC-related domains.
“Low activation, high burden, reduced joy.” May prompt reflection around Depressive, Substance-Related, or OCFCA considerations.
“Marked fluctuation with relational stress.” May prompt reflection around Personality, Mood-related, or Trauma-related domains.

These are not diagnostic mappings. They are narrative prompts for clinicians to explore, always validated against official DSM references, not against E-DSM alone.

E.3 Use Cases

  • Journaling: giving individuals non-clinical language to describe their experience.
  • System Design: informing how Elyon–Sol might adapt tone, pacing, and support style.
  • Education: teaching about stress and resilience without assigning diagnoses.
