Overview

Core Concept Summary

This document captures the core healthcare integration concept for the Elyon-Sol framework: a HIPAA-aligned, non-clinical decision-support and governance system that integrates multi-disciplinary physician expertise via a triadic indexing model called HTI-26.

Compliance

HIPAA Compliance Charter v1.0 (Architectural)

1. Covered Entity Status

Elyon-Sol is not a HIPAA covered entity and not a business associate, because it:

  • Does not store PHI
  • Does not transmit PHI
  • Does not process PHI
  • Does not retain patient identifiers
  • Does not diagnose, treat, or provide medical advice

Elyon-Sol operates strictly as a non-PHI advisory and governance support system.

2. PHI Handling Policy

  • PHI is prohibited from being entered into, processed by, or stored in Elyon-Sol.
  • Permitted inputs: general medical knowledge, guidelines, workflows, and governance logic.
  • Prohibited inputs: any identifiable patient data (names, DOB, MRNs, case specifics, etc.).

Retention: no persistent storage of PHI; all sessions are conceptually PHI-free and non-persistent.

3. Clinical Role Boundary

Elyon-Sol is a decision-support and governance system, not a clinical actor.

Prohibited:

  • Making diagnoses
  • Prescribing medications
  • Interpreting patient-specific labs or imaging
  • Issuing clinical orders

Allowed:

  • Organizing physician knowledge and inputs
  • Structuring and documenting workflows
  • Assisting in drafting protocols and checklists
  • Clarifying reasoning chains for educational or governance use

4. Physician Interaction Rules

Physicians interact with Elyon-Sol as external experts in a strictly advisory capacity.

  • They may provide general, de-identified specialty knowledge.
  • They may review frameworks, logic flows, and policies.
  • They may not provide PHI or case-specific patient data.
  • They may not delegate clinical decisions to Elyon-Sol.

5. Privacy & Security Principles

  • No PHI storage, indexing, or reconstruction.
  • Use of anonymized, generalized medical information only.
  • Strict role and scope boundaries for all users.

6. Alignment with HIPAA

This charter is designed to align with:

  • HIPAA Privacy Rule
  • HIPAA Security Rule
  • Minimum Necessary Standard
  • Non-Covered Entity deployment patterns for advisory tools

Note: This is an architectural and governance alignment, not a legal certification.

Architecture

HTI-26 :: Healthcare Triadic Index

HTI-26 is a physician-integration mechanism based on triads of clinicians, indexed A→Z, designed to safely bring multiple types of medical proficiency into Elyon-Sol without handing clinical authority to AI.

1. Structural Definition

For each letter A–Z, a triad of physicians is defined:

HTI-A = { Physician A1, Physician A2, Physician A3 }
HTI-B = { Physician B1, Physician B2, Physician B3 }
...
HTI-Z = { Physician Z1, Physician Z2, Physician Z3 }

Each triad represents a cluster of expertise, not a fused entity: three peers sharing review and advisory responsibilities.

2. Intended Use

  • Distribute clinical expertise across multiple disciplines.
  • Reduce bias by requiring triad review instead of single-expert dominance.
  • Mirror existing committee and board patterns in hospitals and clinics.
  • Create a scalable, explainable indexing system for up to 78 physicians.

3. Physician Role (Advisory Only)

Each triad functions as a non-clinical advisory unit. Typical responsibilities:

  • Review conceptual care pathways and workflows.
  • Provide input on clinical safety considerations in protocols.
  • Help interpret general evidence and guidelines (in abstract form).
  • Assist in refining logic for alerts, checklists, and decision-support tools (non-patient-specific).

They do not provide patient care via Elyon-Sol.

4. Safety & Governance Features

  • Triadic structure avoids over-reliance on single experts.
  • Advisory-only function keeps decisions in human clinical workflows.
  • Alphabetic indexing (A–Z) provides a simple, scalable namespace.
  • Physician recruitment, vetting, and approval are human-led, not automated.

Executive View

Healthcare Executive Compliance Overview

1. Positioning

Elyon-Sol is positioned as a non-clinical, HIPAA-aligned cognitive framework for organizing multi-disciplinary medical expertise and improving healthcare governance and workflows.

  • Supports physicians, administrators, and governance committees.
  • Does not handle PHI or make medical decisions.
  • Enhances clarity, documentation, and cross-team communication.

2. HTI-26 Value Proposition

HTI-26 (Healthcare Triadic Index) offers:

  • Up to 26 triads (A–Z) with 3 physicians each (78 total clinicians).
  • Distributed expertise and built-in bias reduction.
  • Patterns that resemble existing hospital boards and committees.
  • Flexible mapping to specialties (e.g., Acute Care, Cardiology, Diagnostics, etc.).

3. Compliance & Safety Summary

  • No PHI is stored, processed, or transmitted.
  • Physicians function as external advisors, not as clinical users of an EMR system.
  • System is explicitly non-clinical and non-prescriptive.
  • All use is aligned to non-covered, non-PHI advisory purposes.

Overall Risk Level: Green (for non-PHI advisory use)

4. Example Use Cases

  • Designing and reviewing clinical workflows and protocols (conceptual).
  • Supporting root-cause analysis and safety reviews (de-identified).
  • Creating educational materials and guideline summaries.
  • Assisting interdisciplinary committees with structured reasoning documents.
